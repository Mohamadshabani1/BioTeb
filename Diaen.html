Diabetes

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the body has a difficulty regulating the blood glucose(sugar). Blood Glucose is controlled by an hormone secreted by the Panceras called "Insulin" and it lower the blood Glucose.

Diabetes has 2 types

Type 1

Type 1 diabetes is when the Panceras produces little or no insulin.

Type 2

Type 2 diabetes is when your body cells doesn't use Insulin well and doesn't respond normally to Insulin.

Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes is a chronic autoimmune condition in which the immune system attacks and destroy the insulin producing cells in the pancreas for unknow reasons, This type is common on children and young adults.

Treatment of type 1 diabetes

The main treatment for Type 1 diabetes is daily insulin injection multiples times a day and patients should reduce the intake of sugary food in order to keep the blood glucose within a normal and healthy level.

Symptoms of type 1 diabetes

Excessive Thirst

Excessive hunger

Frequent urination

Weight loss

Fatigue

Blurred vision

Tips for type 1 diabetes patients

Balanced diet

You should adapt a balanced diet including vitamins and nutrients , try to eat vegtables , proteins and focus on whole grain products

Monitor Blood sugar level

Monitor Blood sugar level is recommended for Diabets patients.

Exercise more

Exercising will improve your physical health and it can help improve Insulin sensitivity.

Take care of your mental and emotional health

Stay connected with your loved ones to get better and ask for help if needed

Stay hydrated

Drink water reguarly and avoid soft drinks and caffiene.

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes take place because your Panceras doesn't make enough Insulin or your body cells doesn't use Insulin properly or Both , Type 2 can occur at any age but it is more common on adults

Causes and risk factors of type 2 diabetes

Family history

Age

The risk of type 2 Diabetes increases with age.

Obesity and Unhealthy diet

Symptoms of type 2 diabetes

Excessive Thirst

Excessive hunger

Frequent urination

Weight loss

Fatigue

Blurred vision

Treatment of type 2 diabetes

Treatment of type 2 diabetes includes Oral medications in order to improve cells Insulin sensitivity, and lifestyle modifications such as healthy diet and regular exercises.

Diffrence Between type 1 and type 2

Type 1 diabetes, commonly diagnosed in youth, is an autoimmune condition requiring lifelong insulin therapy due to the immune system attacking insulin-producing cells. On the other hand, Type 2 diabetes, often diagnosed in adults, results from genetic and lifestyle factors, with treatment ranging from lifestyle changes to insulin. The key distinctions lie in age of onset, insulin production, and risk factors.
