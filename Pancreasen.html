Anatomy

The Pancreas

What is the Panceras?

The pancreas is a gland, about six inches long(15cm), located in the abdomen. It is shaped like a flat pear and is surrounded by the stomach, small intestine, liver, spleen and gallbladder.

Main Functions of the pancreas

Exocrine function

Produces enzymes that helps with digestion

Endocrine function

releases hormones (such as insulin) into the bloodstream to regulate sugar levels.

Exocrine function:

The exocrine function of the pancreas involves the production and secretion of digestive enzymes that aid in the digestion of food. These enzymes, including amylase, protease, and lipase, are released into the small intestine via a network of ducts. This digestive process is essential for nutrient absorption and overall digestion.

The exocrine pancreatic enzymes

Amylase

Amylase breaks down carbohydrates into sugars

Protease

Breaks down proteins into amino acids

Lipase

lipase breaks down fats into fatty acids and glycerol.

Endocrine function:

The endocrine function of the pancreas involves the secretion of hormones into the bloodstream, influencing various physiological processes.

The endocrine pancreatic hormones

Insulin

Insulin plays a crucial role in lowering blood sugar levels by facilitating the uptake of glucose by cells, especially muscle and adipose (fat) cells.

Glucagon

glucagon raises blood sugar levels by promoting the release of glucose from the liver into the bloodstream.

What are the parts of Panceras?

The pancreas anatomy is divded into 4 main parts.

Head

The right side of the pancreas, close to the stomach and the first part of the small intestine.

Neck

The narrow part that connects the head to the rest of the pancreas.

Body

The wider middle part, behind the stomach.

Tail

The left side that reaches toward the spleen, an organ on the left.
